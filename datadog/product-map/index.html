Reading the maturity layer

Core — strength. Proven, market-leading, the cash engine. High defensibility, low remaining upside.
Proven expansion. Working land-and-expand; mostly settled, moderate upside.
Contested bet. Challenger in a big market; real upside, unproven monetization.
Optionality. Early, thin, speculative — highest variance, tiny revenue today.

Anchor discipline

HARD (10-K) — total revenue, US / international split, customer cohorts, dollar-based net retention. Datadog reports as one segment, with no per-product financials.

DIRECTIONAL (deck) — everything at product level. Direction yes; absolute numbers into a model, no.

Rule: structure and TAM from here; numbers for a DCF from the 10-K / 10-Q.

How to read positioning

Strength = market leader, defended mainly by the single-platform effect (every signal correlated in one place).

Challenger bet = big market, entrenched rival named in the panel; Datadog wins mostly on consolidation, not features.

Optionality = early land-grab riding the AI or platform-engineering wave; weigh as upside, not base case.
